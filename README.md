# Customer Engagement & Product Utilization Analytics for Retention Strategy

## Project Overview

This project analyzes customer engagement and product utilization patterns in the banking sector to understand their impact on customer retention. The objective is to identify customer behaviors associated with churn and provide actionable insights for retention strategy development.

## Problem Statement

Banks often lack quantitative insights into customer engagement and product usage behaviors that influence retention. As a result, retention strategies are frequently generic and fail to target high-risk customers effectively.

## Objectives

### Primary Objectives

* Evaluate the relationship between customer engagement and churn
* Measure the retention impact of product utilization
* Identify high-value customers at risk of churn

### Secondary Objectives

* Support engagement-driven retention strategies
* Improve product bundling decisions
* Reduce silent churn among premium customers

## Dataset Information

The dataset contains 10,000 customer records and includes:

* CustomerId
* CreditScore
* Geography
* Gender
* Age
* Tenure
* Balance
* NumOfProducts
* HasCrCard
* IsActiveMember
* EstimatedSalary
* Exited (Churn Indicator)

## Analytical Methodology

### Data Validation

* Dataset verification
* Missing value analysis
* Duplicate record checking
* Data type validation

### Customer Engagement Classification

* Active Engaged Customers
* Active Low Product Customers
* Inactive High Balance Customers
* Inactive Disengaged Customers

### Product Utilization Analysis

* Churn by product count
* Single-product vs multi-product comparison
* Product depth impact analysis

### Financial Commitment Analysis

* Balance vs Activity analysis
* Salary vs Balance relationship
* Premium customer risk identification

### Retention Strength Assessment

* Engagement Retention Ratio
* Product Depth Index
* Credit Card Stickiness Score
* Relationship Strength Index

## Key Findings

* Active customers have significantly lower churn rates compared to inactive customers.
* Customers using two banking products demonstrate the strongest retention behavior.
* High account balance alone does not guarantee customer loyalty.
* Customer engagement is one of the strongest indicators of retention.
